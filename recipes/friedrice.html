Yangzhou Fried Rice

Description

Yangzhou fried rice is a popular Chinese wok-style fired rice dish. Commonly sold outside of China as â€œhouse special fried rice. The main difference in Yangzhou fried rice is the use of a combination of proteins. Rather than using a single protein like shrimp or pork or chicken as the dominant ingredient, Yangzhou uses a variety. Most common combination is pork, shrimp and frequently chicken or duck.

Ingredients

  • 5 cup day old cooked rice
  • 3 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked in 2 cups of hot water for 20 minutes
  • 1/3 cup canola oil
  • 4 spring onions, cut into 1/4" slices
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2oz cured Chinese-style ham
  • 1 small carrot, cut into 1/4" pieces
  • 2 tbsp bamboo shoots, cut into 1/4" pieces
  • 1/4 cup green peas
  • 1/4 cup cooked chicken thigh, cut into 1/4" pieces
  • 2 tbsp cooked chicken gizzard, cut into 1/4" pieces
  • 1/4 cup small raw shrimp, peeled
  • 3 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 tsp of salt, to season

Steps/Instructions

  1. Remove the stems of the soaked mushrooms and cut the caps into 1/4" pieces.
  2. Heat a wok over high heat, adding about half the oil.
  3. Fry the spring onion and garlic for about 30 seconds. Add in the shiitake, carrot and bamboo shoots. Fry further for a minute.
  4. Add the chicken thigh, gizzard, peas and shrimp. Season with salt and toss until vegetables are softened and prawns are barley cooked.
  5. Remove from the wok, set aside.
  6. Return the wok to the heat, add the remaining oil.
  7. Add the egg, stirring vigorously to scramble.
  8. When egg is nearly set, add the rice and toss well to coat with pieces of egg.
  9. Separate the clumps of rice by pressing them against the side of the wok with the back of the wok spatula.
  10. Season generously with salt.
  11. Add more oil if necessary.
  12. Return the fried ingredients back to the wok and toss to mix well.
