Gyudon

Description

Gyudon, is a Japanese dish consisting of a bowl of rice topped with beef and onion simmered in a mildly sweet sauce flavored with dashi (fish and seaweed stock), soy sauce and mirin (sweet rice wine). It may sometimes also be served with toppings such as raw or soft poached eggs, Welsh onions (negi), grated cheese or kimchi. A popular food in Japan, it is commonly eaten with beni sh≈çga (pickled ginger), shichimi (ground chili pepper), and a side dish of miso soup.

Ingredients

  • 1# sirloin
  • 3/4 cup dashi
  • 3/4 cup Homemade Teriyaki Sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 brown onion, peeled and thickly sliced
  • cooked short-grain rice, to serve
  • 1/2 cup sliced spring onions, to serve
  • beni shoga, to serve (optional)
  • Japenese Seven-Spice, to serve (optional)

Steps/Instructions

  1. Combine the dashi and teriyaki sauce with the sugar in a medium saucepan and bring to a boil.
  2. Add in the onion and simmer for 2-3 minutes, until the onion has softened.
  3. Add in the sirloin and stir until just cooked.
  4. Season with salt.
  5. Add beef mixture on top of rice, allowing a little stock to soak into rice.
  6. Sprinkle with Seven-Spice and spring onion. Serve with beni shoga
