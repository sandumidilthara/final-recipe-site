Okinawa Taco Rice

Description

Taco rice is a popular example of Okinawan cuisine. It consists of taco-flavored ground beef served on a bed of rice. Frequently served with shredded cheese, lettuce, tomato and salsa. It's one of the best-known dishes of Okinawan cuisine outside of Okinawa. Taco rice is a popular dish among U.S. military personnel stationed in Okinawa as lunch or late night bar food.

Ingredients

  • 1# ground beef
  • 1 pkt taco seasoning
  • 1/2 cup chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 8 cups cooked short-grain rice, to serve
  • 1 cup mild salsa, to serve
  • shredded cheese, to serve
  • shredded lettuce, to serve
  • tomatoes, halved and sliced, to serve

Steps/Instructions

  1. Heat a frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Add ground beef, frying for a few minutes until well browned.
  3. Add taco seasoning, soy sauce and chicken stock. Bring to a simmer, stirring occasionally until the stock is evaporated.
  4. To serve: Place a mound of rice on a large oval plate. Top with ground beef, cheese, lettuce and tomato. Serve salsa on the side.
